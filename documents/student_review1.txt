Rathbone has good food, good variety, and awesome workers, but it gets very packed and it is hard to find a seat.

Brodhead is much smaller and cozier with fewer options. The food is comparable to Rathbone but slightly worse. 
It is farther from freshman dorms and is often closed. It is technically for upperclassmen but freshmen can petition to access it.

Common Grounds in Rauch offers decent sandwiches and snack options when available. It often runs out of items and can be inconsistent. 
The workers are described as chaotic and sometimes unprofessional.

The Grind in FML is popular for sandwiches and pastries and is where many students get coffee. The coffee is cheaper on a meal plan than 
external cafes but quality is average.

Hideaway Cafe in HST offers yogurt bowls and smoothies. It is generally considered average.

Global Cafe is moving into Hawks Nest and offers sushi, tea, and smoothies. It often has limited availability, closes early, and 
sometimes stops serving food before official closing times. Staff primarily focus on retail.

Food trucks like Fud Truk, Purple Pita, and Talon serve some of the best food on campus. However, they are inconsistent and often 
unavailable during bad weather, Fridays, or weekends.

Hawks Nest currently has several retail options but is being remodeled and will lose most of them. It is heavily criticized for 
long wait times, incorrect orders, and poor food quality. Wait times of 40 minutes for fries are not uncommon and food quality is 
often described as poor.

UC Asas Place offers good breakfast food, but there are concerns about food safety and reports of people getting sick.

South Mountain Deli is widely considered bad and is known among students for raw chicken issues.

Leaf and Ladle offers soups and salads and is generally good but very inconsistent. Some orders are heavily over-seasoned or 
poorly prepared.

Simple Servings offers basic bowl-style meals. It is generally not well liked.

Kalamata serves gyros and bowls. It started strong but declined significantly in quality. Some gyro meat is still acceptable 
but overall quality is poor.

Hearth offers pizza and pasta. Pizza quality declines over time and pasta is considered acceptable but often worse than other 
options like Rathbone.

The Pub is expensive, often costing over 40 dollars per person. The meal deals are small but taste decent and the atmosphere is good.

Clutch and Go is a robot-style convenience food system. It is expensive, does not accept swipes, and has inconsistent pricing issues.

Overall, the food situation on campus is frustrating. Many places are closed on weekends or at inconvenient times. Food safety
concerns like raw chicken and mold are mentioned by students. Healthy options like fresh fruit are limited. Students often rely on Rathbone or 
food trucks, and the dining experience is generally stressful and inconsistent.

Simple Servings is for people with allergies. It’s free of the top 9. While it could be bland for those who can 
eat everything, it’s vital for those with restrictions.
