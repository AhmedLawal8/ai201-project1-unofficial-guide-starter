I've been coming to Rathbone Dining Hall for over five years. I've seen it at its best, average, and most disappointing. My overall rating 
is 2 stars. The food is often not cooked properly, lacks seasoning, but salads are a reliable option.

Rathbone operates as an all-you-can-eat buffet with a wide range of stations, which sounds great on paper. There is a lot of variety, but 
consistency is lacking across meals.

The Healthy Choice station usually includes dietary-restricted foods, fish, pork, vegetables, and international dishes. The food here is 
generally under-seasoned, but that is expected for this station.

The Vegetarian Choice station usually includes tofu, mushrooms, stir-fry, vegetables, pita, hummus, and other vegetable-based foods.

The Featured Meal station is usually a protein, vegetable, and starch combo such as steak tips, mashed potatoes, and green beans.

The pasta station includes noodles, garlic bread, Alfredo sauce, and red sauce. Sometimes they serve tortellini, lasagna, and meatballs.

The pizza bar includes thick crust pizza with toppings like pineapple, ham, basil, pepperoni, sausage, and mushrooms. Seasonings like 
parmesan and red pepper are available. Quality is decent but not comparable to typical East Coast pizza.

The grill station includes grilled chicken, fries, burgers, hotdogs, and chicken sandwiches. It is similar to the featured meal but focused 
on grilled items.

The special grill offers beef tips, chicken cubes, and mac and cheese bowls with toppings like bacon, broccoli, tomato, and mushroom. The mac 
and cheese is generally well liked.

This station includes wraps, sandwiches, cold cuts, cheeses, vegetables, and condiments. It also includes soups and sometimes chili, 
plus yogurt and granola.

The dessert station includes apple pie, jello, pudding, cakes, muffins, cookies, six ice cream flavors, and waffle machines with toppings 
like Nutella and peanut butter.

This includes Pepsi products, teas, fruits like apples, oranges, pears, bananas, and a cereal, bagel, and bread station with a toaster and 
microwave.

Rice is often overcooked and mushy. Pizza is usually not warm unless freshly made. Chicken quality is inconsistent and often questioned by 
students. Salad bar layout is inconvenient and restocking is inconsistent. Ice cream flavors run out quickly.

There is a lot of variety at Rathbone, but consistency is the main issue. Food quality and seasoning are inconsistent, and execution often 
feels minimal rather than refined. Food quality significantly impacts student experience on campus.
