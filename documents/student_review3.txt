The lehigh meal plan is not worth the 3k, but it’s not bad. The main dining hall is Rathbone. There are healthy options there, 
especially in the allergy-free section. Rathbone has almond and soy milk dispensers, which is a nice touch.

Inside Rathbone you’ll find a salad bar, sandwich-making area, burger grill, soup station, and an ice cream and dessert section, 
along with rotating hot entrées each day, so there’s usually a lot of variety. It also has a custom pasta bar, waffle machines, 
a solid vegetarian/vegan section, and an omelet station for breakfast. Comfort foods like pizza and fries are almost always available too.

There are more than enough meal options overall, but the required freshman meal plan is still overpriced (like it is at most schools). 
If you get to know the chefs, they’re sometimes open to feedback and suggestions—people have even gotten them to expand vegan options based 
on requests.

Outside of Rathbone, there are other places on campus where you can use meal swipes, so it’s worth checking Lehigh’s dining site for the 
full list.
